About

I'm a final-year CSE student at American International University-Bangladesh, working at the intersection of explainable AI, multimodal deep learning, and low-resource language processing.

My research spans Bangla NLP and commonsense reasoning, medical-imaging classification, biomedical forecasting with large language models, and sustainable computing — usually with SHAP, LIME, or Grad-CAM doing the explaining. I currently split my time between Elite Research Lab (New York, remote) and Learnify Research Lab (Dhaka), building datasets, baselines, and reproducible pipelines that other researchers can actually use.

Research Focus

What I keep coming back to

Four threads run through most of my work, regardless of which dataset or domain it's applied to.

01

Explainable AI

Using SHAP, LIME, and Grad-CAM to make deep models — from Bangla commonsense reasoning to chest X-ray classifiers — show their reasoning, not just their predictions.

02

Multimodal Deep Learning

Fusing vision, text, and tabular signals for tasks spanning hand-gesture recognition, fire detection, and medical image analysis.

03

Bangla & Low-Resource NLP

Building datasets and benchmarks — including XCommonsense-BN and Bangladesh-focused misinformation detection — for one of the world's most under-served major languages.

04

LLMs for Biomedical Reasoning

Benchmarking models like BioGPT and FLAN-T5 against decades of real biomedical evidence, from mRNA vaccines to CRISPR-Cas9 therapy.
